Algorithmic Visibility

Opacity, Curation, and Feedback Loops

Algorithms are the invisible hands that curate our digital experiences. From social media feeds to search results, these systems determine what content is surfaced and what remains hidden. The logic behind these decisions is often opaque, making it difficult for users to understand why they see certain posts or recommendations.

This opacity is not just a technical issue—it has real consequences for user agency and knowledge. As Naheyan et al. (2024) note, algorithmic curation and dark patterns can shape user experience and decision-making, often without the user’s full awareness (Naheyan et al., 2024). When algorithms prioritize engagement or profit, they may reinforce existing biases, amplify sensational content, or silence minority voices.

Algorithmic systems also create feedback loops: the more users interact with certain types of content, the more similar content is shown to them. This can lead to filter bubbles, where users are exposed only to information that aligns with their existing beliefs. Understanding these dynamics is crucial for navigating digital environments critically and responsibly.

“Dark patterns and algorithmic curation can shape user experience and decision-making, often without the user’s full awareness.”
